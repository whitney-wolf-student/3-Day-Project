1950s - 1970s : The Beginning

  • 1958: Tennis for Two is created by William Higinbotham.
  • 1962: Spacewar! becomes the first known computer game.
  • 1972: The first home video game console, Magnavox Odyssey, releases, with Pong by Atari.

1980s

  • 1983: The Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) starts revolutionizing gaming.
  • 1985: Super Mario Bros. popularizes platform gaming.
  • 1989: Sega Genesis and Nintendo's Game Boy introduce new home consoles and handheld gaming.

1990s

  • 1994: Sony enters the market with the PlayStation, introducing CD-based gaming.
  • 1996: Nintendo 64 introduces 3D gaming with Super Mario 64.

2000s

  • 2001: Microsoft debuts the Xbox.
  • 2004: World of Warcraft popularizes online multiplayer gaming.
  • 2006: Nintendo Wii introduces motion controls, while PlayStation 3 and Xbox 360 push HD graphics.
  • 2009: Minecraft introduces open-world sandbox gaming.

2010s

  • 2013: Sony’s PlayStation 4 and Microsoft’s Xbox One define the generation.
  • 2017: Nintendo Switch revolutionizes hybrid gaming.
  • 2018: Fortnite leads the battle royale genre.
  • 2019: Call of Duty: Warzone and Apex Legends keep online competitive gaming in focus.

2020s

  • 2020: PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X deliver ultra-realistic graphics and fast load times.
  • 2023: The Legend of Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom and Baldur's Gate 3 dominate as top-performing games.
  • 2023+: VR becomes a mainstream form of gaming and is rapidly evolving.
